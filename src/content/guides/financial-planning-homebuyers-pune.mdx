## Home Loan Eligibility & EMI Planning

Your home loan eligibility is primarily dictated by your FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio). Banks typically prefer that your total EMI obligations do not exceed 50% of your net monthly income.

**Pro Tip:** To increase your loan eligibility for a premium 3 BHK, consider applying for a joint home loan with your spouse. This not only pools your incomes but also allows both applicants to claim individual tax benefits.

## Fixed vs Floating Interest Rates

The eternal debate for homebuyers. 
*   **Fixed Rates:** Offer predictability but are usually priced 1-2% higher than prevailing floating rates. They also carry hefty prepayment penalties.
*   **Floating Rates:** Fluctuate with the RBI's repo rate. While your EMI might vary slightly over the years, historical data shows floating rates are more economical over a 15-20 year tenure. Furthermore, RBI mandates that floating rate loans have **zero prepayment penalties**, allowing you to aggressively pay down the principal whenever you receive annual bonuses.

## Maximizing Tax Benefits & Concessions

Do not leave money on the table. The Indian government incentivizes real estate investment through several mechanisms:
1.  **Section 80C:** Up to ₹1.5L deduction on principal repayment.
2.  **Section 24(b):** Up to ₹2L deduction on interest payments.
3.  **Female Concession:** The Maharashtra government offers a flat 1% discount on stamp duty if the property is registered solely or jointly in a woman's name. On a ₹2 Cr luxury apartment, this is an instant saving of ₹2 Lakhs.
